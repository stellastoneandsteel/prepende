2026-06-15 · later

Reliability has a lever — and a low ceiling correction on the record

New ablation (n=80) — exact-optimum hit-rate vs lock strength: a weak sub-harmonic injection lock (Ks=0.6) gives ~0%; the standard lock (Ks=1.8) ~9%; the strongest tested (Ks=2.4) ~10%. The gain from Ks=1.8 to 2.4 is within sampling margin at this n and should not be read as confirmed headroom above the standard lock — the real lever is the weak→standard jump. So reliability is partly tunable, but bounded. The ceiling is the result: even at the strongest lock the machine still misses the exact optimum ~90% of the time at this scale. Our earlier read — "reliability is structural, not tunable" — was too strong; the corrected position is that lock strength gives a modest, real uplift while problem size stays the dominant governor. We post it because it refines an earlier claim in the open — that correction, on the record, is the point.

See also: the animated build model — where this hit-rate becomes the restart multiplier R in the energy estimate.

2026-06-15

OIM scaling & ablation · gap-reservoir [T2] pre-registered MISS · [T1] estimate

Oscillator Ising machine (Build A) — scaling + ablation software, measured

Method: simulated OIM ODE vs simulated annealing on random Max-Cut (p=0.5), matched restarts. Code: sim_oim_scaling.py; data: data/oim-scaling.json.

  • Best-cut quality ties SA up to n=120 nodes (OIM/best 100% at n≤60 → 99.75% at n=120; SA 100% throughout).
  • Single-solve quality and exact-optimum reliability are one finding, not two. Mean per-restart cut ≈ 99% of the best (a per-restart mean, not best-of-k) — while the probability a single restart hits the exact optimum collapses from 91.7% of restarts (55/60: 2 graphs × 30 restarts) at n=20 nodes to 5% at n=80 and 0% at n≥100 nodes. (Throughout, n is node count and hit-rate is per-restart.) High per-solve quality with zero exact-optimum reliability at scale means ensemble restarts are required at scale.
  • Robustness (quality only): that ≈99% per-restart mean held across 4× SHIL strength (Ks 0.6–2.4) and 4× noise (0.3–1.1) at n=80 — near-optimal cut quality is insensitive to these over the tested range. It does not extend to exact-optimum hit-rate, and the breaking point was not located (a wider sweep is a noted next step).
Energy — read first. The OIM ODE in software is slower than SA on the same CPU — a measured result of this study, not a hedge. No energy was measured. Any energy-efficiency case is a hardware argument about projected analog dissipation, not demonstrated here. Wherever "energy win" appears below it means: if a physical bench were built and ran near component limits, it might dissipate less per solution — two unverified conditionals.
  • Restart-cost: because exact-optimum hit-rate collapses, energy must be counted per verified solution, not per run; at n ≳ 40, 3–10× restarts are routine and multiply any per-run figure.
  • Scope: results apply to the tested instance classes only (random Max-Cut, p=0.5, n≤120). Generalization to larger or structurally different instances is not established. No general-solver-competitiveness claim is made.

Gap-coupled reservoir [T2] — pre-registered miss

Pre-registered at p=0.45 in the ledger, locked before running (git history proves the order). Test: gap-coupled 2×N reservoirs (readout dim N) vs a single 2N reservoir at matched readout dim N; signal-recovery R² across noise {0.1,0.3,0.5,0.7}, 5 seeds. Code: sim_gap_reservoir.py.

Result: gap mean R² 0.269 vs single (matched readout) 0.962; the gap collapses with noise (0.525 → 0.10). Hypothesis not supported; point estimate contrary. Resolved y=0. The pre-registration leaned against the hypothesis and was correct — this is informative, not null.

[T1] physical bench — estimate

This is an estimate, not a measurement. Estimated probability a physical op-amp coupled-oscillator bench shows a real energy-per-solution win vs SA-on-CPU at matched cut quality: ≈0.18. (The locked forward prediction stands at p=0.30; restart-cost analysis revises the working estimate down to ~0.18 — but the locked prediction will still be scored as locked, not retrofitted.) Forecast: per-run analog energy looks favorable (mW over µs), but restart multiplicity at scale likely consumes or reverses it on a per-verified-solution basis; a clean, reproducible win against a well-tuned CPU SA baseline is unlikely without a substantial improvement to solution reliability.
